INTRODUCTION

What is the EB-2 NIW Visa?

The EB-2 National Interest Waiver (NIW) visa is an employment-based visa that allows foreign nationals with exceptional abilities in arts, sciences, or business, as well as those holding advanced degrees in these fields, to bypass the standard labor certification process required for most employment-based visas. Here are the key features of the EB-2 NIW visa:

No Employer Sponsorship Required

Individuals can self-petition for the EB-2 NIW visa, eliminating the need for a U.S. employer or sponsor to apply on their behalf.

National Interest Waiver

The EB-2 NIW visa grants a waiver of the standard labor certification process. This means you don't need to prove that there are no qualified U.S. workers available for the position you're seeking.

Self-Petition Option

Unlike most employment-based visas, the EB-2 NIW visa doesn't necessitate a job offer from a U.S. employer. Instead, you must demonstrate that your work will be in the national interest of the United States.

Faster Processing Times

Because the EB-2 NIW visa skips the labor certification process, its processing times are often quicker than other employment-based visas.

How Do You Qualify for an EB-2 NIW?

To qualify for an EB-2 NIW visa, you must meet certain requirements that demonstrate exceptional ability in the sciences, arts, or business, and that your work is in the national interest of the United States. Requirements include the following:

Exceptional Ability

You as the applicant must possess exceptional ability in the sciences, arts, or business. This can be demonstrated through meeting any 3 of this:

  • Official academic record showing that you have a degree, diploma, certificate, or similar award from a college, university, school, or other institution of learning relating to your area of exceptional ability
  • Letters from current or former employers documenting at least 10 years of full-time experience in your occupation
  • A license to practice your profession or certification for your profession or occupation
  • Evidence that you have commanded a salary or other remuneration for services that demonstrates your exceptional ability
  • Membership in a professional association(s)
  • Recognition for your achievements and significant contributions to your industry or field by your peers, government entities, professional or business organizations
  • Other comparable evidence of eligibility is also acceptable.

Advanced Degree

Alternatively, you may have an advanced degree, such as a master's or doctorate, in your field of expertise.

You must demonstrate that your work is in the national interest of the United States. This can be shown by proving that your work will have a significant impact on the U.S. economy, health, education, culture, or welfare.
You must provide evidence that your work has value to the country, such as letters of recommendation from experts in your field, publications, patents, or media coverage of your work
National Interest

Self-Petition

You must self-petition for the EB-2 NIW visa, meaning you do not need an employer or sponsor to apply on your behalf.


Positive Discretion

You must demonstrate that granting the waiver would be in the national interest of the United States and that you are well-positioned to advance work in your field.


Waiver of Labor Certification

The applicant must also request a waiver of the labor certification process. This typically requires employers to demonstrate that there are no qualified U.S. workers available for the position being filled.


Is EB-2 NIW Difficult to Get?

Obtaining an EB-2 NIW visa can be challenging. This is because it requires meeting strict requirements and demonstrating exceptional ability in the sciences, arts, or business, as well as showing that the applicant's work is in the national interest of the United States. Here are some ways in which obtaining an EB-2 NIW visa can be difficult:


High Standards

The standards for exceptional ability in the sciences, arts, or business are high. It can be difficult to demonstrate that the applicant meets these standards. This can require extensive documentation of achievements, such as degrees, awards, publications, and other evidence of exceptional ability.
